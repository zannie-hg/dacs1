About Us

Vietnam has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that has been shaped by its history, traditions, and the influence of neighboring countries. The country's culture is a unique blend of indigenous customs and practices, along with Chinese, French, and Southeast Asian influences.

Vietnamese culture places a strong emphasis on familial and social relationships, respect for elders, and community values. Family plays a central role in Vietnamese society, and filial piety is highly regarded. Ancestors are revered, and rituals and ceremonies are performed to honor them.

Traditional Vietnamese architecture is characterized by its harmony with nature and the use of natural materials such as bamboo, wood, and clay. Notable examples include the ancient town of Hoi An, Hue's Imperial City, and the wooden pagodas found throughout the country.

Vietnamese cuisine is renowned worldwide for its fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and balance of textures. The cuisine is diverse, with regional specialties such as pho (noodle soup), banh mi (baguette sandwich), and bun cha (grilled pork with noodles) gaining international recognition.

Vietnamese Ao Dai

For women, the Ao Dai typically consists of a tight-fitting, long-sleeved silk tunic that reaches down to the knees. The tunic is worn over loose-fitting pants called "Dam Beo" or a long skirt called "Vay." The tunic is often tailored to be form-fitting, accentuating the curves of the wearer. Women's áo dài often feature intricate embroidery, delicate patterns, and vibrant colors.

Tet Nguyen Dan

Tet, or the Lunar New Year, is the most important and widely celebrated festival in Vietnam. It marks the arrival of spring and involves family reunions, special meals, giving and receiving lucky money, and various customs and traditions.
